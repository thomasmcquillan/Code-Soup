Make/Use is made possible by the collaborative efforts of a talented team of designers who bring a wealth of experience from various disciplines. They use their unique skills talents to educate and inspire. Here we share some highlights from the Make/Use project.

Make/Use Film

Jason O'Hara

Jason developed and produced the Make/Use film and cognitive videos. For the film, he conceptualised a sequence that evoked the construction of the Make/Use garments, with a model enticing the garments to form around her while she dances. His production team used the latest in high-speed video camera technology and innovative thinking to capture a balance of edge and elegance. The Make/Use project’s zero waste philosophy is an extension of Jason’s ongoing research into humanity’s relationship with nature and each other.   Please note: The video contains flashing lights!

Starring Hannah Dellow. Directed by Jason O'Hara. Production and Editing by Jason O'Hara and Monish Patel. Garments designed by Holly McQuillan with textiles by Greta Menzies.

Digital Draping with CLO3D

Holly McQuillan

Make/Use creator and project-lead Holly McQuillan began using the 3D Design software CLO3D as a way to reduce fabric waste in the early stages of the garment design process. She has since made it a central part of her design process, allowing her to 'drape' the digital fabric upon a virtual model - using the software as a tool with which to 'sketch' and design. Watch the video below to get a glimpse into the process of designing a tshirt in CLO3D.
